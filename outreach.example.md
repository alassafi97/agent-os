## Offer Positioning

How to describe what you sell — in one sentence, used as the anchor in every email/DM.

> We build custom AI systems that automate the repetitive operational work — prospecting, outreach, follow-up, internal workflows — so your team focuses on what actually grows the business.

## Social Proof

Specific results agents can reference. Use real numbers. Update as you close new clients.

- "[X]+ clients worked with"
- "[X]x average ROI in first [N] days"
- "[X]+ hours saved per client per month"
- "$[X]M+ in costs eliminated across clients"
- "[N]-day deployment — from first call to live system"
- Notable clients: [Client 1], [Client 2], [Client 3]
- [X]+ people trained / webinar attendees

## CTA Preferences

What the ask should be at each stage. Agents will use these exact CTAs (or close variations).

- **Email 1 (soft):** A question, not a meeting request. Examples: "Is this something you're thinking about?" / "Worth a conversation?" / "Curious if this is on your radar?"
- **Email 2 (medium):** Offer to show something. Examples: "Happy to walk through what that looks like" / "Want me to send the case study?"
- **Email 3 (hard):** Direct but respectful close. Examples: "If it's not a priority, no worries — just didn't want you to miss it." / "Either way, I'll stop here."
- **LinkedIn connection:** Never a CTA. Just a reason to connect.
- **DM 1:** Question about their situation. Never pitch.
- **DM 2:** Offer a resource or insight. Soft bridge to what you do.
- **DM 3:** Direct ask for a call. Break-up energy if no response.

## Tone

Default: **Direct and casual.** Like a peer who respects their time.

Adjust per channel:
- Email: direct, confident, short
- LinkedIn: slightly warmer, more conversational, peer-to-peer

Do NOT sound like:
- A salesperson
- A recruiter
- A corporate newsletter
- An AI

DO sound like:
- Someone who's done this before
- Someone who doesn't need the deal
- Someone who's sharing something useful, not selling

## Angles

Default angles to use when personalizing outreach. Agents pick the best fit per prospect.

1. **Headcount ceiling** — "You're growing but can't keep hiring proportionally. AI handles the scale."
2. **Manual bottleneck** — "Your team is spending X hours on stuff that should be automated."
3. **Referral dependency** — "You're growing on referrals alone. What happens when they slow down?"
4. **Competitor pressure** — "Your competitors are automating outreach. You're still doing it manually."
5. **New leadership** — "New CEO/VP inheriting old processes. Fresh eyes = fresh systems."
6. **Hiring signal** — "You're hiring for X. That role could be an AI agent instead."

## Banned Phrases

Agents MUST NOT use any of these. Non-negotiable.

### Words
leverage, streamline, synergy, cutting-edge, game-changer, revolutionary, innovative, delighted, thrilled, empower, unlock, transform, disrupt, scale (as a verb without context), optimize (without specifics)

### Openers
- "I hope this finds you well"
- "I came across your profile"
- "I was impressed by"
- "I noticed that you"
- "I wanted to reach out"
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "I'm reaching out because"
- "My name is X and I"

### Follow-ups
- "Just following up"
- "Just checking in"
- "Bumping this"
- "Circling back"
- "Touching base"
- "Per my last email"

### Closers
- "I'd love to pick your brain"
- "Let's connect and explore synergies"
- "Would love to get on your calendar"
- "Looking forward to hearing from you"
- "Please don't hesitate to reach out"

### Fake Personalization
- "I noticed you're in [industry]" — that's not personal
- "As a fellow [title]" — unless you actually share the role
- "Companies like yours" — name the specific company
- "Your impressive background" — say what specifically

## Industry-Specific Notes

Add notes for industries you sell into frequently. Agents use these for deeper personalization.

### Staffing / Recruiting
- Pain: reps spend more time sourcing than selling
- Hook: "Your team is doing 30 manual touches/day. AI does 200+ with better personalization."
- Proof: "3x meeting rates in 60 days for a staffing firm your size"

### SaaS / Tech
- Pain: founder-led sales doesn't scale
- Hook: "When you're heads-down on product, pipeline dries up."
- Proof: "Automated outbound generated $200K pipeline in 90 days"

### Professional Services / Consulting
- Pain: referral dependency, feast-or-famine pipeline
- Hook: "Referrals are great until they slow down."
- Proof: "Built an outbound engine that landed 3-5 meetings/week without adding headcount"

### Franchise Operations
- Pain: inconsistent follow-up across territories
- Hook: "48 territories, one follow-up system. Every lead gets the same response in under 5 minutes."
- Proof: "Email deliverability from 40% to 92% across all franchise territories"
